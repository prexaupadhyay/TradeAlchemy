Stock Market Fundamentals

Verified, educational insights to start your trading journey.

What Are Stocks?

A stock represents an investment method to build wealth, meaning you own a share in the company that issued the stock. Companies raise money on the stock market by selling these ownership stakes to investors.

Common Stocks

  • Offer higher growth potential but come with more risk.
  • Provide investors with voting rights at shareholder meetings to have a say in corporate decisions.

Preferred Stocks

  • Provide a steady income stream and greater security.
  • Come with priority in dividend payments and asset distribution in the event of liquidation.

Trading Styles

Day Trading

You buy and sell stocks multiple times a day, usually selling everything off before the end of the trading day.

Swing Trading

You hold investments for days or weeks to capitalize on upticks or swings in the market.

Position Trading

Relies on long-term growth, where investors hold their position for many months or years.

Key Technical Indicators

Traders use technical indicators to analyze market activity and make informed decisions.

  • Moving Average (MA): An indicator used to identify the direction of a current price trend, without the interference of shorter-term price spikes. It calculates the average stock price over a given period.
  • Relative Strength Index (RSI): A technical momentum indicator that compares the magnitude of a stock's average gains and losses. It is expressed as a figure between 0 and 100, where a reading around 70 is often considered overbought, and near 30 is considered oversold.
  • Volume: Indicates how many shares are exchanged over a certain time period, providing insight into the strength, conviction, and dependability of a price trend.

Risk Management

Essential Rule: One of the most important things to remember when trading is to manage your risk by only risking a small percentage of your account on each trade. Additionally, utilizing a stop-loss order automatically triggers a sale at a price you define, which can help safeguard you from losing more than you agree to.
